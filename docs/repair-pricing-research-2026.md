# Phone-Repair Parts Costs & Pricing Benchmarks (2025–2026)

> **Deep-research report — scoped for Nuera Tech (Guelph, Ontario)**
> Compiled June 2026. Multi-source, adversarially fact-checked.

**Question:** What do common iPhone & Samsung **screen, battery, and charge-port** repairs cost — both in **wholesale parts** (what a shop pays) and **retail pricing benchmarks** (what manufacturers and shops charge) — as of mid-2026, with a Canada/Ontario lens?

**Confidence legend:** 🟢 High (primary source or multi-source agreement) · 🟡 Medium (single credible/secondary source) · 🔴 Flagged (weak / conflicting / inferred)

**Method note & honest limitation:** Live B2B distributor cart prices (MobileSentrix, Injured Gadgets, Mobile Defenders) are **login-gated and return 403 to crawlers**, so per-unit *wholesale* numbers below come from publicly-priced resellers (eTech Parts, Repairs Universe, RepairPartsUSA, iFixit) — these run **at or slightly above true bulk/account pricing**, so treat them as a *ceiling* on your part cost. Manufacturer retail figures were verified against Apple's and Samsung's own pages (Apple's JS pricing widget doesn't render to crawlers, so those were corroborated via launch-day press + Apple's legal/newsroom pages).

## 1. TL;DR — the five things that matter

1. **Parts are cheap; you sell labor + warranty + trust.** A battery part costs a shop **~$10–18 USD** (aftermarket) to **~$45–60** (genuine-grade), yet retails at **$70–150 CAD**. The gross margin on repairs lives in labor, not parts.
2. **Screen tier is the single biggest cost lever** — aftermarket/incell vs. genuine OLED is a **2–4× swing** in both part cost and what you can charge. A two-tier ("economy / OEM-quality") screen menu is the industry norm and serves both price-shoppers and quality-seekers.
3. **Samsung flagships are the expensive screen jobs; iPhone charge ports are the expensive board jobs.** Samsung fuses glass + OLED (+ frame) into one pricey module; iPhone charge-port damage can drag in a *soldered* logic-board connector → microsoldering.
4. **Canada adds ~37–40% to USD part prices via FX alone** (USD/CAD ≈ 1.38), before shipping/tax. This structurally compresses margins vs. US shops — sourcing strategy matters.
5. **Genuine Apple parts became buyable in Canada in Aug 2025** (via MobileSentrix). This is a real competitive/positioning opportunity for an independent shop.

## 5. Charging-port repair (iPhone + Samsung) — the most misunderstood job

> **The core price driver (verified via iFixit teardown):** On **Samsung**, the USB-C port is soldered to a **separate, swappable daughterboard** → a plug-in board swap, **no microsoldering**, cheap & predictable. On **iPhone**, the port is a flex assembly connecting to the logic board via a **soldered FPC connector** → if the connector/pads/charging IC (Tristar/U2) are damaged, it becomes a **microsoldering** job. 🟢

### Three labor tiers

| Tier | What it is | Typical price |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Clean-only | debris / lint removal | ~$25 |
| 2. Plug-in flex / sub-board swap | 20–45 min | **iPhone $50–190 USD** / **Samsung $50–125 USD**; **Canada ~$80–150 CAD** (newer iPhone) |
| 3. Logic-board microsoldering (iPhone) | scope + hot-air rework | **$150–$300 USD** |

### Wholesale parts 🟡

- **Samsung sub-board:** ~$8–25 USD (UK example: full PCB w/ USB-C = £13.99).
- **iPhone flex:** aftermarket ~$4–25 USD; iFixit genuine iPhone 15 charge port = **$62.99**.

### Canada retail & the Apple catch 🟡

- **Cell N Tech (Canada):** newer iPhone port **$80–150 CAD** (replacement up to $250); premium Android $80–120 CAD.
- 🔴 **Gap:** no clean *Ontario per-model charge-port CAD price list* found publicly — recommend pulling a couple of live KW/Guelph quotes to calibrate.
- **Apple doesn't do a cheap port fix** — charging faults fall under "Other Damage" / whole-unit service: **CAD $719–$919** for the iPhone 14 line. **Samsung Canada doesn't publish a port price** (quote-only). *This is a clear win column for independents.* *(Sources: iFixit S23 guide, FoneFunShop, iPhone Repair 4 Less, iMobile Detroit, Cell N Tech, iPhone in Canada)*

## 6. The Canadian cost-structure overlay (what makes your math different)

| Factor | Detail | Confidence |
|---|---|---|
| **FX** | USD/CAD ≈ **1.38** (June 2026; 2026 YTD avg ~1.3737). A US$100 part ≈ **CAD $137–139** *before* shipping/duty/tax — a structural ~37–40% uplift. | 🟢 |
| **Import de minimis** | Courier from US under CUSMA: **tax-free ≤ CAD $40**; **$40.01–150 duty-free but GST/HST applies**; **>$150 full duty + tax**. Most parts orders exceed $150 → favor Canadian-stocking distributors. | 🟢 |
| **Tariffs** | Canada **removed counter-tariffs on most US imports Sept 1, 2025** (CUSMA-compliant goods); steel/aluminum/autos still tariffed. **No special tariff targets phone parts** — FX + sales tax are the real cost pressure, not duties. | 🟢 |
| **Canadian distributors** | MobileSentrix Canada, Canadian Cell Parts, Cellphone Parts Canada, eSourceParts, Mengtor — Canada-wide shipping, CAD pricing, but **wholesale prices are reseller-login-gated**. | 🟢 |
| **Genuine Apple parts** | **Aug 19, 2025** Apple expanded **Self Service Repair + Genuine Parts Distributor** to Canada — independents can now buy genuine iPhone/iPad displays, batteries & charging ports **via MobileSentrix**. *(Verified verbatim, Apple CA newsroom.)* | 🟢 |

## 7. Right-to-repair & regulatory outlook

- **Federal Bill C-244** — Royal Assent **Nov 7, 2024**; legalizes circumventing TPMs *for repair*. **But** it still bars *offering circumvention services* and *making/importing/distributing circumvention tools* — so its practical benefit to shops is limited. 🟢
- **Quebec Bill 29** — first Canadian consumer right-to-repair law, **in force Oct 5, 2025** (phased). **Quebec-only; Ontario has no equivalent in force** as of June 2026 — but it signals the regulatory direction and pressures makers to supply parts. 🟢
- **EU Battery Regulation (EU) 2023/1542** — user-replaceable batteries required **from Feb 18, 2027**. ✅ **Correction:** the real exemption covers **wet-environment / washable devices**, and **IP rating alone is *not* sufficient** to claim it — the popular "IP67 + durability-cycles" exemption was **withdrawn-proposal text, not the enacted law**. Doesn't bind Canada, but drives global phone design (one hardware SKU worldwide). 🟢
