01 — How to use this

One source of truth — now light & dark

Nuera Tech ships light and dark themes from one token set — a near-black ink canvas or a soft off-white canvas, both carrying the same vivid purple, cyan and green accents. Every value here is a real token shipping in the app, so what you design matches what users see.

About the CMYK values: these are calculated sRGB→CMYK estimates for layout and comps only. For anything going to press, match to a defined ICC profile or a Pantone bridge — and for the near-blacks, specify a proper rich black (≈ C60 M50 Y40 K100) rather than the literal estimate. The neon accents will read brighter on screen than any CMYK build can print.

04 — Light & dark themes

One palette, two canvases

Nuera Tech ships both themes from a single token set. The app follows the operating system by default and remembers a persistent override via the header toggle. The brand DNA — the purple→cyan signature, action green, tight Inter rhythm — stays constant; only the canvas and the exact accent values change.

Dark canvas

  • Page sits on Ink 900 #07070c (raised surfaces on Ink 800 #0c0d16).
  • Accents run at full vibrancy: purple #8b5cf6, cyan #22d3ee.
  • Gradient stops: #c4b5fd → #8b5cf6 → #22d3ee.
  • The default for OLED-friendly, app-like, tech-forward moments.

Light canvas

  • Page sits on a soft off-white #f6f7fb (raised surfaces on #eef0f7).
  • Accents darken for AA legibility: accent #7c3aed, cyan #0e7490.
  • Gradient stops: #7c3aed → #6d28d9 → #0e7490.
  • Same hierarchy and ink text (#0e1222) for bright, daylight reading.

Why the accents shift: the neon purple and cyan that sing on ink would fail contrast as small text on a pale canvas. In light, the system uses a darkened accent ramp (#7c3aed / #0e7490) so the same brand identity still clears WCAG AA — re-tuned, never inverted.

07 — Brand psychology & vibe

Vibrant, but premium

Neon purple and cyan on a near-black canvas read as tech-forward and app-like; the desaturated trust-slate neutrals and tight Inter hierarchy keep it feeling premium and trustworthy rather than gaming. Green signals trust, savings and the WhatsApp booking action. The personality is confident, honest, fast and hyperlocal to Guelph — matching the promise of transparent, always-cheaper repair.

The product's own design note says it best: "the premium, not gaming feel comes from the trust neutral + tighter rhythm + clearer hierarchy + smoother easing, not from desaturation … vibrancy IS the brand."

08 — Colour usage rules

What dominates, what highlights

Ink backgrounds and near-white text carry the canvas. Purple is the lead brand accent; cyan is the co-accent and gradient endpoint. Green, amber and red are functional highlights — used sparingly and with meaning.

Do

  • Let Ink 900 / Ink 800 own most of the surface area.
  • Use the purple→cyan gradient for primary actions and brand moments.
  • Reserve green for booking, savings and success states.
  • Use the trust-neutral slate for icons and credibility cues.

Don't

  • Don't tint large backgrounds with amber or red.
  • Don't use green as a decorative brand colour.
  • Don't place purple text on ink below ~16px without checking contrast.
  • Don't place the neon accents as small text on light — use the darkened accent ramp the light theme defines.
09 — Logo designer brief

How to apply colour to the new logo

A targeted colour brief for the new mark. The creative direction is open — evolve the current "N" or start fresh — but the colour rules below keep it unmistakably Nuera.

Core colours (1–3)

Lead with Brand Purple #8b5cf6 and Brand Cyan #22d3ee, optionally bridged by Purple Light #c4b5fd, expressed as the signature gradient. Action Green is not a logo colour — it stays reserved for CTAs and booking.

Monochrome & grayscale

The mark must hold up as a single flat colour. The primary one-colour lockup is a white knockout (#f4f6ff) on dark; use full ink (#07070c) for light or print. Map grayscale to the neutral ramp (#f4f6ff → #9fa9bd → #8794ab → #07070c). Never rely on the gradient for legibility.

Contrast & placement

The logo lives on near-black UI, so it must pop on Ink 900 / Ink 800: target ≥ 3:1 for the graphic mark and ≥ 4.5:1 for any wordmark text. Keep clear-space of at least the height of the "N", set a sensible minimum size, and don't drop the bare gradient onto busy aurora areas without the badge container.
